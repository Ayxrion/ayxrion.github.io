Although Horlick is ranked rather low, there are still some benefits at Horlick High School. For example, Horlick has one of the most aggressive sports teams in Wisconsin. Despite being old, the building has lived up quite well, and still recieves expansions and refinishing.

History

The name "Horlick H.S." was named after the "William Horlick," who was the original patent holder for malted milk. He donated the land for the school 10 years before the patent. In Sept. 17th, 1928, the doors were first opened to students. It wasn't officially dedicated for students up until October-November 1928, and released as a public school in January, 1929. In 1929, the school had 33 rooms, 16 full-time teachers, 7 part-time teachers, and an enrollment of 407 students.
